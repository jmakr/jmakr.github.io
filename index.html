{{ page.title }} Jap-Nanak Makkar is a Ph.D. candidate at the University of Virginia, Department of English. She researches, teaches, and presents on the history of technology and the global Anglophone novel. Her dissertation, Global Narrative in Electronic Modernity, argues that the global novel offers an aesthetic correlate for a broader social transformation underway in the applied and theoretical sciences. Some of her research is forthcoming from boundary 2 (“More on the Missing ½ Second: A Review of Hansen [2000, 2015] and Hayles [1999, 2012]”). She has presented excerpts of her dissertation at ACLA 2016, MLA 2017, Narrative 2017 and at the Clinton Institute for American Studies.
