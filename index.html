About

Jap-Nanak Makkar is a doctoral candidate at the University of Virginia, Department of English, where she researches and teaches global Anglophone literature.

Her dissertation, “Technology Changes the Stories We Tell: Digital Technology and Media, Global Narratives, 2000-2015,” examines global literature in light of post-1945 technological history. Her work is forthcoming from boundary 2. In fall 2016, she was awarded a HASTAC 2016-2018 fellowship.

She received a B.A. (2010, Hons.) and M.A. (2011) from the University of Toronto.
