About

Jap-Nanak Makkar is a doctoral candidate at the University of Virginia, where she researches and teaches in global Anglophone literature. Her dissertation, “Technology Changes the Stories We Tell,” examines global literature in relation to the recent history of technological advancement.

Her work is forthcoming from boundary 2.

She received a B.A. (2010, Hons.) and M.A. (2011) from the University of Toronto.
